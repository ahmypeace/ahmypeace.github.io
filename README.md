# Amaka Peace Onebunne

Responsible AI | AI Safety | Computational Social Science
Media & Communication Researcher

Advancing computational approaches to understand AI, information, and society.

I study how artificial intelligence transforms the ways people communicate, make decisions, and produce knowledge. My research combines computational methods with communication scholarship to examine trust, governance, information ecosystems, and the societal consequences of emerging technologies.

Working across Human–Machine Communication, AI governance, and computational social science, I investigate how digital systems redistribute power and shape experiences across diverse social and cultural contexts, particularly in the Global South.

## Selected Work

### Journalists' Perception of Artificial Intelligence: The Case of Nigerian Newsrooms
**Master's Thesis**

A qualitative study exploring how Nigerian journalists perceive, adopt, and negotiate artificial intelligence within newsroom practice. Through in-depth interviews with journalists across Nigerian news organizations, this research examines AI as collaborator, tool, and institutional challenge, highlighting issues of trust, cultural context, newsroom routines, and human–machine collaboration.

**Keywords:** Human–Machine Communication, Journalism, AI Adoption, Newsrooms, Trust, Nigeria

---

### Algorithmic Gaze and the Feedback Loop of Othering
**Conference Paper**

A computational investigation of how recommendation algorithms reproduce and amplify stereotypical representations of Africa across digital platforms. Using large-scale visual content analysis, the study demonstrates how algorithmic recommendation systems reinforce historical narratives and shape global perceptions through feedback loops of engagement and visibility.

**Keywords:** Algorithmic Bias, Recommendation Systems, Africa, Computational Media, Platform Studies

---

### Algorithmic Bias and Media Manipulation: A Systematic Review
**Review Article**

A systematic review synthesizing interdisciplinary research on algorithmic bias, media manipulation, and platform governance. The paper maps the mechanisms through which algorithmic systems influence information exposure, amplify inequalities, and shape public discourse, while identifying emerging directions for responsible AI research.

**Keywords:** Algorithmic Bias, Media Manipulation, Responsible AI, Information Ecosystems, Digital Platforms

---

### Sociotechnical Imaginaries of AI Expansion: Constructing the Global South as Speculative Market
**Conference Paper**

An analysis of how governments, technology companies, and policy actors frame the Global South as the next frontier for AI deployment. Drawing on theories of sociotechnical imaginaries, the project examines how narratives of innovation, investment, and development shape AI governance, technological dependency, and global power relations.

**Keywords:** AI Governance, Global South, Sociotechnical Imaginaries, Digital Colonialism, Political Economy

---

### AI News Framing in Africa
**Working Paper**

A computational analysis of artificial intelligence coverage across African newspapers. By combining natural language processing with communication theory, the project identifies dominant narratives, framing patterns, lexical differences, and geopolitical variations in how AI is represented across countries and media systems.

**Keywords:** News Framing, Computational Communication, NLP, AI, African Media

---

### Deepfake Discourses in African News Media
**Working Paper**

A cross-national computational study examining how African newspapers report on deepfakes. The research investigates dominant frames, recurring themes, institutional actors, and linguistic patterns to understand how synthetic media is constructed as a technological, political, and societal issue.

**Keywords:** Deepfakes, Computational Text Analysis, Media Framing, Political Communication, Africa

---

### *I Said What I Said* and Beyond: Parasocial Intimacy, Pragmatic Feminism, and the Digital Re-scripting of Nigerian Womanhood
**Conference Paper**

An exploration of Nigerian podcasting as a communicative space where women negotiate identity, intimacy, and contemporary gender expectations. The project investigates how conversational podcasts foster parasocial relationships, digital sisterhood, and evolving performances of Nigerian womanhood.

**Keywords:** Podcasts, Digital Feminism, Parasocial Relationships, Nigerian Media, Gender

---

### Responsible AI Corporate Framing
**Ongoing Research**

A computational analysis of AI governance documents from leading technology companies to examine how organizations define responsibility, safety, evaluation, and accountability. The project explores the discursive construction of "responsible AI" and how governance narratives differ across institutions.

**Keywords:** Responsible AI, Corporate Governance, AI Policy, Trust & Safety, Computational Discourse Analysis
