# Amaka Peace Onebunne  
AI and Media Researcher | Computational Governance | Human–Technology Interaction  

I study how technological systems shape human experience, with a focus on how artificial intelligence redistributes responsibility, risk, and meaning across institutional and social contexts.

My work sits at the intersection of natural language processing, AI governance, and communication research. I build computational pipelines to analyze how organizations describe, justify, and assign accountability in AI systems, and how these descriptions translate into real-world implications for users.

---

## Research Focus

- AI governance and accountability  
- Computational analysis of policy and organizational documents  
- Human interaction with automated systems  
- Global technology systems and uneven data infrastructures  

---

## Selected Projects

### AI Governance Text Analysis  
Computational analysis of Responsible AI documents from leading technology organizations.

- Built NLP pipelines for large-scale text processing  
- Applied topic modeling to identify governance themes  
- Developed a scoring system for obligation and commitment language  
- Analyzed how responsibility is distributed across users, organizations, and systems  

**Outcome:** Reveals how accountability is framed, often shifting between enforceable commitments and aspirational language.

---

### Document Classification and Scoring System  
Designed and implemented a system that automatically flags and rates documents based on key governance and risk indicators.

- Automated classification using text features  
- Integrated rule-based and statistical scoring approaches  
- Designed for analyzing policy, communication, and institutional texts  

**Outcome:** Enables structured evaluation of how documents communicate responsibility, risk, and compliance.

---

### Text Cleaning and Preprocessing Pipeline (peace_genie_pro)  
Developed a reproducible text cleaning pipeline for preparing raw textual data for computational analysis.

- Standardizes encoding and formatting  
- Removes noise such as URLs, emails, and irregular symbols  
- Produces clean, analysis-ready corpora  

**Outcome:** Improves consistency and reliability across downstream NLP tasks.
